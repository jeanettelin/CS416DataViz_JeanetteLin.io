Ratio of Male vs Female Olympians

Olympics 1908 - 1948

In the early years of the Olympics, the representation of Olympians was heavily skewed towards males, reflecting the deeply ingrained societal norms and gender roles of the time. The first modern Olympic Games held in Athens in 1896 saw a limited participation of female athletes, and it wasn't until the 1900 Paris Games that women were officially allowed to compete. However, the number of female participants remained significantly lower than their male counterparts. This underrepresentation of women in the Olympics mirrored the wider gender disparities prevalent in society, where women faced numerous barriers in pursuing sports and were often confined to traditional roles as wives and mothers. The limited opportunities for female athletes were a stark reflection of the social attitudes and expectations that characterized the pre-World War II era.
