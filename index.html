Ratio of Male vs Female Summer Olympians Throughout History

Olympics 1908 - 1948

In the early years of the Olympics, the representation of Olympians was heavily skewed towards males, reflecting the deeply ingrained societal norms and gender roles of the time. In 1908, women made up only 1.5% of all Olympian contestants. By the first half of the 20th century, in 1948, females still only made up less than 10% of Olympic Athletes.

From 1939 - 1945, we saw a pause in the Olympics Games due to World War 2. We will soon see that after this war, there is a turning point for the representation of women in the Olympics, as World War 2 was the start of redefining gender norms in many countries. From this point forth, we will see the ratio of women grow in the Olympic Games
